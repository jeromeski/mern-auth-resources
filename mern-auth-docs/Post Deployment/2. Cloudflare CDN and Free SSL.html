Using Cloudflare CDN and Free SSL

In this article I will guide you through the steps of using Cloudflare's globally distributed CDN (content delivery network) service and free SSL.



Using Cloudflare CDN

  1. With cloudflare CDN, which is a free service, you will also get a benefit of superchaging your app in terms of speed

  2. Your site will be served globally by cloudflare CDN destinations throughout the globe

  3. So what are we waiting for? Lets do it

  4. First create a account/signup to cloudflare

  5. Now login to cloudflare

  6. You will be landed in dashboard/home

  7. Type your domain name and click the button that says Add a Site

  8. If your site is brand new you might see error that says domain not found

  9. But just click the button anyways. it works.


Select a free plan and click Confirm Plan


  1. Cloudflare will automatically detect A record

  2. Towards the end of the page, click Continue


Now that we are using cloudflare Nameservers, we need to replace the Digital Ocean nameservers we entered in Namecheap Dabhboard > Nameservers section (covered in the precious article)


Replace old Digital Ocean Nameservers in Namecheap to the ones you got from Cloudflare

Believe it or not, thats all.


Now in Cloudflare you can click on the button that says Done, check nameservers


It will take few hours for cloudflare to serve your site through their Nameservers

So check back in few hourse (around 2-4 hours in general)


Our site will be serverd by Cloudflare CDN globally but visually you will not see any differences.


Add free SSL by Cloudflare


  1. While waiting lets add fee SSL by cloudflare so that your site is secure

  2. Instead of http, your site will have https. Awesome!

  3. Once cloudflare SSL is applies, it will have such url https://mern-stack.com/

  4. In cloudflare dashboard, click on SSL/TLS

  1. Currently it is set at Full.

  2. Unless you have SSL certificate installed in digital ocean it will not work.

  3. So change it to the option that says Flexible
